Why This Matters Right Now

Windows 10 Has Lost Its Safety Net

Microsoft has officially ended free security updates for Windows 10. That single change has a much bigger impact than most people realise. It does not mean your computer suddenly stops working — it means every security hole discovered from this point forward will never be patched. Every week that passes, a Windows 10 machine becomes a slightly easier target for the people who write viruses, ransomware and scam software for a living.

We are already seeing the effects of this across Perth. Customers who put off the upgrade are coming to us months later with infections that would not have happened on a fully patched, supported system. The good news is that fixing this properly is fast, affordable, and something we can usually sort out the same day you bring your computer in — or even remotely in many cases.

Worth Knowing

What Happens If You Wait

We are not in the business of scaring people into spending money. But we do see the pattern repeat itself constantly in Canning Vale and the surrounding suburbs, and it is worth being upfront about it.

What we commonly see happen on unsupported Windows 10 machines
  • A new strain of malware exploits a vulnerability that will now never be patched on Windows 10
  • The computer becomes noticeably slower as security software has to work harder to compensate
  • Banking and shopping websites start flagging the browser as outdated or insecure
  • New software and printer drivers stop being built with Windows 10 in mind
  • A small problem turns into an expensive repair because the system had no protection left

None of this is guaranteed to happen overnight. But every week on an unsupported system adds risk that simply does not exist once you are properly upgraded. The earlier you sort it, the less likely you are to be dealing with an infection or a failure down the track.
