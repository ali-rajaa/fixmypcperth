Computer Running Extremely Slow
Takes five minutes to open a browser, freezes mid-task, or gets noticeably slower every week. Usually caused by malware, a full or failing hard drive, or not enough RAM for current Windows demands.
Virus, Pop-Ups or Browser Hijacked
Constant ads appearing, homepage changed without your input, searches redirected, or a security warning locking your screen. Your machine almost certainly has malware and needs a professional clean.
Computer Will Not Turn On
Pressing the power button does nothing, the screen stays black, or the machine shuts off immediately after starting. Could be a power supply, battery, motherboard, or overheating fault.
Laptop Screen Cracked or Dead
Cracked panel, lines running through the display, half the screen dark, or the backlight gone completely. Most laptop screens can be replaced same-day with the right part in stock.
Files Lost or Drive Not Detected
Accidentally deleted photos, a drive showing as unformatted, or a hard drive that clicks on startup. Data recovery with a no fix, no fee guarantee starting from $120.
Windows Crashing or Blue Screens
Frequent blue screen errors, error messages at startup, or Windows stuck in a repair loop. Often caused by a failing drive, corrupted system files, or a problematic Windows update.
Computer Overheating or Fan Very Loud
Fan running at full speed constantly, laptop burning hot to the touch, or the machine shutting itself down from heat. Dust buildup inside the case is one of the most common issues we see from Thornlie homes.
Locked Out of Windows
Cannot access your own computer after a Windows update, PIN stopped working, or a Microsoft account is no longer accessible. We recover access the same day in almost all cases.

Thornlie is one of the largest residential suburbs in Perth's south-east. The suburb has its own train station on the Thornlie line, a strong sense of local identity, and a population that relies on computers and laptops for everything from remote work and small business administration to online schooling and keeping in contact with family.

One of the most common issues we see from Thornlie residents is a machine that has picked up malware over time — often through free software downloads, suspicious email attachments, or a browser extension that turned out to be more than it claimed. These infections slow computers down significantly, redirect internet searches, and in some cases generate fake security alerts designed to scare people into paying for unnecessary software. A thorough virus removal, proper antivirus setup, and a full browser cleanup resolves this in most cases the same day.

We also see a high volume of older computers from Thornlie households that are not broken — they are simply exhausted by age. An ageing spinning hard drive is the single biggest performance bottleneck in any machine manufactured before 2018. Replacing it with a modern SSD is one of the most cost-effective repairs we offer. A machine that used to take eight minutes to start up will boot in under fifteen seconds after an SSD upgrade, and the transformation is immediate. Many Thornlie customers tell us the upgrade felt like buying a new computer at a fraction of the price.
