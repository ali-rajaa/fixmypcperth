Sound Familiar?

Common Computer Problems We Fix for Huntingdale Residents

Many computers in Huntingdale homes have been running since the mid-2000s or early 2010s — and while the owners are perfectly happy with what their machine does, Windows has kept demanding more from the same old hardware. The result is a computer that now takes ten minutes to boot, freezes at the worst moment, or throws up alarming error messages nobody understands. Below are the most common issues we see from the Huntingdale area.

Computer Takes Forever to Start Up
Boots so slowly you make coffee first. Usually caused by a failing spinning hard drive that simply cannot keep pace with modern Windows. An SSD replacement typically fixes this completely — and transforms the machine.
Virus, Fake Alerts or Browser Taken Over
Pop-ups claiming your computer is infected, your browser home page has changed, or you are getting redirected to sites you never asked for. Active malware that needs professional removal, not just a scan with the built-in Windows tools.
Computer Won't Turn On at All
Dead screen, no power light, or it powers up for two seconds then shuts off. Could be a failed power supply, dead battery in a laptop, overheating issue, or — worst case — a motherboard fault. We diagnose it free.
Laptop Screen Cracked or Flickering
A cracked panel from a drop, dead pixels spreading across the screen, half the display dark, or a flickering backlight that makes working impossible. Most panels can be replaced same-day once we have the part.
Photos and Files Disappeared or Drive Dead
Accidentally deleted files, a hard drive that Windows can no longer read, or a drive that is clicking and grinding. Our data recovery service runs on a no fix, no fee basis — you pay nothing if we cannot get your data back.
Blue Screen Errors or Random Crashes
BSOD errors at random or during specific tasks, restart loops, or Windows constantly running a startup repair that never finishes. Common causes include failing drives, corrupted system files, faulty RAM, or a bad Windows update.
Overheating, Fan Always Screaming
Laptop burning your legs, fan on full throttle all day, or the machine shutting down mid-task from heat. Years of dust compacted inside the heatsink and vents is the single most common cause — a clean-out and thermal paste refresh usually fixes it entirely.
Locked Out of Windows or Microsoft Account
A Windows update changed something and now your PIN doesn't work, or you can't access your Microsoft account and the machine won't let you in at all. We recover access without wiping your files in most cases, same-day.
