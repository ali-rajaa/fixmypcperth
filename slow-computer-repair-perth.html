Signs Your Computer Needs a Speed Fix

Computers slow down gradually, so it is easy to accept the frustration as normal. It is not. These are the signs Perth locals bring their PCs in for, and every one of them is fixable.

Boot Takes 5+ Minutes
Waiting for Windows to load long after you press the power button. Often caused by a dying hard drive, too many startup programs, or both at once.
Freezes and Locks Up
The mouse stops moving, programs stop responding and you sit there waiting. Can be RAM issues, overheating, a failing drive, or malware consuming all available resources.
Fans Running at Full Speed
The PC sounds like it is about to take off even when you are doing nothing intensive. Usually a sign of dust-blocked cooling, causing the CPU to overheat and throttle its performance.
Browser Crawls and Lags
Web pages take forever to load, tabs freeze, and scrolling is jerky. Can be a RAM shortage, malware running in the background, or a hard drive that cannot keep up.
PC Gets Worse Over Time
A PC that was fast three years ago and is now unbearably slow. Years of software installs, background processes, and startup entries accumulate into a system choking on its own clutter.
Slow and Unresponsive After Virus
Had a virus recently, ran an antivirus tool, but the PC is still sluggish? Many infections leave behind damaged system files or hidden processes that continue to drag performance long after the initial removal.
Root Causes

Why Is Your Computer So Slow? The Real Reasons.

Most slow PC problems have a specific, diagnosable cause. We have repaired hundreds of slow computers across Perth's southern suburbs and the same culprits come up again and again. Here is what we look for when your machine comes in.

01
Failing or Slow Hard Drive
Mechanical hard drives slow dramatically as they age. Bad sectors cause the drive to retry reads repeatedly, grinding performance to a halt. This is the single most common cause of a genuinely slow PC in older machines. An SSD upgrade solves it instantly and permanently.
02
Startup Program Overload
Every program you install wants to run at startup. After years of installs, dozens of applications are fighting for CPU and RAM the moment Windows loads. The result is a machine that crawls for the first 10 to 20 minutes every time you turn it on. We strip this back to what actually needs to run.
03
Overheating and Thermal Throttling
Processors are designed to reduce their clock speed automatically when they get too hot. If dust has blocked the heatsink or the thermal paste has dried out, the CPU runs hot constantly and throttles itself to avoid damage. The result is a PC that runs at a fraction of its actual capability. A proper thermal clean and repaste fixes it completely.
04
Malware and Background Infections
Adware, cryptominers, and spyware all run hidden processes that consume CPU, RAM, and internet bandwidth. Many people scan with a basic antivirus, get told the PC is clean, and still have hidden processes running. We use professional-grade tools that go deeper than consumer antivirus software to find and remove what is actually causing the drain.
05
Insufficient RAM
If your PC has 4GB of RAM and you are running Windows 11 with a browser, Office, and a few background apps open, the system is constantly swapping data to the hard drive to compensate. This is called thrashing, and it is brutally slow. Upgrading RAM is one of the most cost-effective speed improvements available on older systems.
06
Corrupted or Outdated Drivers
Corrupt or severely outdated drivers cause performance issues, freezing, and instability that mimic hardware problems. Particularly common after Windows updates that break compatibility with older drivers. We check every driver on the system and update anything that is causing issues.
07
Years of Software and Registry Clutter
Uninstalled programs leave behind folders, registry entries, scheduled tasks, and background services that continue to run and consume resources. After five or more years of normal use a Windows installation accumulates enormous amounts of this clutter. A thorough clean can recover significant performance without needing to reinstall Windows.
08
Windows Update Problems
Windows Update running out of control, stuck in a loop, or repeatedly failing can consume CPU and disk resources for hours without the user realising. A botched update can also leave Windows in a degraded state that causes ongoing slowness. We check update status as part of every diagnostic.
Not sure which one is causing your slowdown? That is exactly what our diagnostic is for. We test your machine systematically and tell you precisely what is wrong before we fix anything. You get a straight answer, not guesswork.

Honest Advice

Tune-Up, Upgrade, or Reinstall — What Does Your PC Actually Need?

Not every slow PC needs the same fix. We will always tell you what your specific machine actually needs — not whatever costs the most. Here is a plain English guide to the options and when each one makes sense.

A tune-up alone ($89) is usually the right starting point when the PC is relatively recent — three to five years old — and the slowdown has crept up gradually over time. Startup bloat, software clutter, a basic malware clean, and a thermal dust out will often recover most of the lost performance without any hardware changes. If the diagnostic reveals the drive and RAM are healthy, this is likely all that is needed.

A tune-up plus SSD upgrade ($89 + from $65 labour) is the most transformative combination available for machines that are still solid hardware but are running on an old mechanical hard drive. The difference is not subtle — it is the difference between a machine that takes five minutes to boot and one that boots in under twenty seconds. We clone your existing drive so nothing is lost. This combination typically costs less than $200 total and extends the usable life of the machine by several years.

A tune-up plus RAM upgrade is the right call when the diagnostic shows the machine is regularly running out of physical memory and resorting to virtual memory on the drive. Very common on machines that shipped with 4GB of RAM that are now running Windows 11 and a modern browser. Adding RAM is inexpensive and eliminates the constant thrashing that makes these machines feel unresponsive under normal workloads.

A Windows reinstall ($120) becomes the better option when the slowdown is caused by deep Windows corruption, a severe malware infection that has damaged system files, or a machine that has accumulated so much clutter over many years that a targeted clean would take longer than starting fresh. We will recommend this honestly when it applies and combine the pricing where services overlap.

If the hardware is genuinely too old to be worth repairing, we will tell you that too. We would rather give you an honest assessment and lose the job than take $200 to repair a machine that will frustrate you again in three months. We see machines every week where the honest recommendation is replacement, and we say so.
