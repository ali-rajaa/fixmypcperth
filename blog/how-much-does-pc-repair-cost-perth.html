Factors That Influence Laptop Repair Costs

While software fixes are usually fixed-price, hardware repairs like laptop screen repair in Perth are influenced by several factors:

  • Part Availability: Common models like Dell Inspiron or HP Pavilion have cheaper, readily available parts. MacBooks or Microsoft Surface devices require premium components.
  • Complexity: Replacing a keyboard on an old Dell takes 10 minutes. On a modern MacBook, it requires removing every single internal component.
  • Urgency: Some shops charge a "Priority Fee" of $50-$100 for same-day service. (We try to avoid this at FixMyPC Perth!).
Pro SEO Tip: Always check if your technician offers a "No Fix, No Fee" guarantee. This ensures you aren't paying for someone to "try" and fail to fix your device.

Onsite vs. Workshop Repairs

Many Perth services offer onsite computer repair where they come to your home or office. While convenient, this is often the most expensive way to fix a PC because you are paying for the technician's travel time.

Bringing your device to a dedicated workshop (like ours in Canning Vale) is usually 30-40% cheaper because we can work on multiple machines simultaneously while scans are running, passing those savings on to you.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Is it worth fixing a 5-year-old laptop?

In most cases, yes. A 5-year-old laptop with a slow hard drive can be made "better than new" with an SSD upgrade for around $180 (parts and labour included). This is much cheaper than spending $1,200 on a new machine.

Do you charge a diagnostic fee?

Many Perth shops charge $50-$80 just to look at the computer. At FixMyPC Perth, we offer free basic assessments. If we can see the problem immediately, we won't charge you to tell you what it is.

How long does a typical repair take?

80% of our repairs in Canning Vale are completed within 24 hours. Hardware parts that need to be ordered from over East (Melbourne/Sydney) can add 2-3 days to the wait time.
