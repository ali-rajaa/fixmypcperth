How Much Does PC Repair Cost in Perth? (2026 Guide)

Ali Raja · FixMyPC Perth · 20 April 2026 · 5 min read · 📍 Canning Vale, Perth

Finding out the cost of a computer repair can feel like pulling teeth. Many shops in Perth won't give you a straight answer until you've already paid a "diagnostic fee." I believe in total transparency. While every job is unique, most repairs fall into predictable price brackets.

Whether you're in Canning Vale, Thornlie, or the Perth CBD, here is a realistic breakdown of what you should expect to pay for quality computer service in 2026.

Important: These prices are for labour only. If your repair requires hardware (like a new screen or power supply), that will be added to the total.

1. General Software & Maintenance ($80 – $130)

This is the most common type of service. If your computer is running slow, acting "glitchy," or needs a clean-up, it usually fits here. These services generally take 1–2 hours of work.

  • Virus & Malware Removal: Expect to pay around $90 – $120. This should include a full deep-scan and system optimization.
  • Operating System Reinstall (Windows/macOS): Usually $100 – $130. A fresh start is often the best "fix" for a messy computer.
  • General Tune-up: Around $80 to clear out junk files and speed up boot times.

2. Hardware Upgrades & Repairs ($90 – $150)

Hardware work requires physical disassembly and often careful handling of static-sensitive components. Prices vary depending on how difficult the device is to open (laptops are usually more expensive to work on than desktops).

  • SSD Upgrade (Labour): Around $100. This usually includes "cloning" your old data so you don't lose anything.
  • Laptop Screen Replacement: Labour is usually $90 – $140 plus the cost of the screen (screens vary from $80 to $300+).
  • RAM Installation: Simple jobs are often $40 – $60 if done standalone, or free if part of a larger service.
Pro Tip: At FixMyPC Perth, I offer fixed-price quotes. That means if a job takes longer than expected, you don't pay a cent more than what we agreed on.

3. Complex Issues & Data Recovery ($150 – $300+)

When things go seriously wrong, the labour involved increases. This category covers deep-level troubleshooting where the cause isn't immediately obvious.

  • Liquid Damage Repair: This is unpredictable but often starts at $180 for a full ultrasonic cleaning of the motherboard.
  • Standard Data Recovery: Usually $120 – $250 for logical errors (deleted files or corrupted drives).
  • Gaming PC Custom Builds: $150 – $250 depending on the complexity (liquid cooling vs air cooling).

Beware of "Hourly Rates"

Some shops in Perth charge $120+ per hour. The problem with hourly billing is that it rewards slow work. If a technician spends three hours struggling with a simple virus, you shouldn't have to foot the bill for their learning curve.

Tip: Always ask for a "Fixed Price" or a "Capped Quote" before handing over your machine.
