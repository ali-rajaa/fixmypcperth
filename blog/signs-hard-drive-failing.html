5 Signs Your Hard Drive Is About to Fail (Don't Ignore These)

Ali Raja · FixMyPC Perth · 12 April 2026 · 6 min read

Hard drives are the silent engines of our digital lives. Whether you are running a business in the Perth CBD or storing years of family photos in Canning Vale, your hard drive is likely the most fragile component in your computer. Unlike your RAM or your CPU, traditional hard drives (HDDs) have moving parts that spin at thousands of rotations per minute.

When these parts wear out, the results are catastrophic. Hard drive failure is one of the leading causes of data loss in Western Australia. However, drives rarely die without a warning. If you know how to spot the signs of a failing hard drive, you can perform a backup and save yourself thousands in professional data recovery fees.

1. The "Click of Death" & Mechanical Noises

If you notice your laptop or desktop making unusual sounds, it is the most immediate red flag. A healthy hard drive should be nearly silent or make a very faint whirring noise. If you hear clicking, grinding, screeching, or repetitive buzzing, the mechanical arm inside the drive is likely failing to find its track.

Urgent Warning: If you hear a clicking sound, power off your computer immediately. Continuing to run the drive can physically scratch the data off the internal platters, making recovery impossible.

2. System Slowdowns & Frequent Freezing

Many Perth locals mistake a failing hard drive for a "virus" or just an old computer. If your PC takes 10 minutes to boot, or if Windows Explorer freezes when you try to open a simple folder, your drive is likely struggling to read bad sectors.

As the magnetic surface of the disk degrades, the computer has to try over and over again to read a single file. This "retry" process is what causes your mouse to turn into a loading circle and your apps to stop responding.

3. Corrupted Files & Disappearing Data

Have you ever opened a document only to see garbled text? Or perhaps a photo that only loads halfway? File corruption is a primary symptom of a drive that can no longer maintain the integrity of its stored data. In some cases, files or even entire folders might simply disappear from your desktop. This isn't a glitch; it's a sign the drive's file system is crumbling.

4. S.M.A.R.T. Status Errors

Modern computers are equipped with S.M.A.R.T. (Self-Monitoring, Analysis, and Reporting Technology). Sometimes, upon booting your PC, you may see a black screen with a message like: "Pri Master Hard Disk: S.M.A.R.T. Status BAD, Backup and Replace."

Do not ignore this. Your computer’s motherboard has detected that the drive has exceeded its internal failure thresholds. It is literally telling you it is about to die.

5. The "Blue Screen of Death" (BSOD)

While a Blue Screen can be caused by software, frequent crashes—especially when accessing files or installing new programs—often point to hardware failure. If you see the error code "KERNEL_DATA_INPAGE_ERROR", there is a very high probability that your hard drive is the culprit.

The Solution: Upgrade to an SSD

If your hard drive is failing, the best path forward is an SSD Upgrade. Solid State Drives (SSDs) have no moving parts, meaning they are much more durable and up to 10 times faster than old mechanical drives. At FixMyPC Perth, we specialize in cloning your old failing drive onto a new SSD, often keeping all your files, settings, and programs exactly where you left them.

Summary Checklist

  • Back up now: Use a cloud service (Google Drive/OneDrive) or an external USB.
  • Avoid stress tests: Don't run "Defrag" or "Chkdsk" if you suspect physical failure.
  • Seek help: If the data is critical, let a pro handle the cloning process to minimize risk.
