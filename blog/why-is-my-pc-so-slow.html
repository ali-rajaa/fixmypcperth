Why Is My PC So Slow The 7 Most Common Causes and Fixes

Ali Raja · FixMyPC Perth · 15 May 2026 · 6 min read · Canning Vale, Perth

A slow PC is the number one complaint I hear from customers in Canning Vale and across Perth. And nine times out of ten, the fix is simpler than people expect. Here are the seven most common reasons your PC has slowed down and exactly what you can do about each one.

Quick note: Most of these issues are fixable at home for free. But if you have tried the basics and your PC is still crawling, message me on WhatsApp — most speed issues are sorted same day.

1. Too many programs starting with Windows

Every time you install software, it often adds itself to your startup list. Over months and years this builds up. Your PC ends up spending 3 to 5 minutes loading 30 programs you did not even know were running before you can do anything useful.

Fix it: Press Ctrl + Shift + Esc to open Task Manager, click the Startup tab, and disable anything you do not recognise or do not need immediately. Be careful not to disable antivirus or drivers.

2. Your hard drive is almost full

Windows needs free space to operate, it uses your drive as temporary memory. When you are below 10 to 15 percent free space, performance tanks dramatically. This is especially bad on older spinning hard drives.

Fix it: Open File Explorer, right click your C: drive and check how much space is free. Delete old downloads, empty the recycle bin, and uninstall programs you do not use. If you are consistently full, a storage upgrade might be worth it.

3. You are still running a spinning hard drive (HDD)

This is the single biggest performance upgrade you can make. Old spinning hard drives are 5 to 10x slower than modern SSDs. If your PC takes 3 or more minutes to boot, this is almost certainly why.

Real example: A customer in Thornlie had a laptop that took 4 minutes to boot. After an SSD upgrade, it booted in 9 seconds. Same laptop, same Windows, just a different drive.

Fix it: An SSD upgrade with full data transfer starts from $80 labour only, or from $150 if you need the SSD supplied too. It is the best money you will spend on an older PC.

4. Malware or a virus running in the background

Malware constantly uses your CPU and RAM behind the scenes, sometimes to mine cryptocurrency, sometimes to send spam, sometimes just to wreak havoc. If your PC suddenly got slow, this is worth checking.

Fix it: Run a free scan with Malwarebytes (the free version is fine for a one off scan). If it finds threats, or if the problem persists after removal, a professional clean is the safer option.

5. Not enough RAM

If you are running Windows 11 with 4GB of RAM, you are going to have a bad time. Modern Windows needs at least 8GB to run comfortably, and 16GB if you are doing anything beyond browsing and email.

Fix it: Press Ctrl + Shift + Esc, click Performance, then Memory. If you are consistently above 80 percent RAM usage while doing normal tasks, an upgrade will make a noticeable difference. RAM upgrades start from $70 labour only.

6. Overheating causing throttling

When a CPU or GPU gets too hot, it automatically slows itself down to prevent damage. This is called thermal throttling. If your PC feels like it starts fine but gets slower the longer you use it, heat is often the culprit.

Fix it: Clean the dust out of your case (compressed air works well) and make sure all fans are spinning. If the problem continues, the thermal paste between your CPU and cooler may need replacing, this dries out over 3 to 5 years and causes temperatures to spike.

Warning: Never use a vacuum cleaner inside your PC, it generates static electricity that can damage components. Use compressed air only.

7. Windows needs a clean reinstall

Windows accumulates junk over years, broken registry entries, corrupted files, leftover software debris. Sometimes the best fix is a fresh start. A clean Windows install with your files backed up can make a 5 year old PC feel new again.

Fix it: A full Windows reinstall with data backup starts from $100. All your files come back safely, just Windows gets wiped and rebuilt from scratch.
