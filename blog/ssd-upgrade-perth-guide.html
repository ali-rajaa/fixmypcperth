SSD Upgrade Guide: How Much Faster Will Your PC Get

Ali Raja · FixMyPC Perth · 5 May 2026 · 7 min read · Perth WA

If your computer takes several minutes to turn on, stutters when you open Chrome, or sounds like it is grinding away inside, you do not necessarily need a new PC. In fact, swapping out an old hard drive for a Solid State Drive (SSD) is the single best upgrade you can make.

I perform dozens of SSD upgrades every month in Canning Vale, and customers consistently tell me their 6 year old laptops feel faster than the day they bought them. Here is everything you need to know about the difference an SSD makes.

What is the difference between an HDD and an SSD

Traditional Hard Disk Drives (HDDs) have been around for decades. Inside, they look like a tiny record player, using a mechanical arm to read and write data on spinning magnetic platters. Because they rely on moving parts, they are physically limited in how fast they can fetch your files.

A Solid State Drive (SSD), on the other hand, uses flash memory similar to what is inside your smartphone. There are absolutely zero moving parts. This means data is written and accessed instantly, silently, and with much less battery drain on laptops.

The Real World Speed Difference

When we talk about speed, the numbers speak for themselves. Even the most basic, entry level SSD is roughly 5 to 10 times faster than a standard mechanical hard drive. If you install an NVMe SSD (a newer, smaller type of SSD), it can be up to 35 times faster.

Real Results: I recently upgraded a customer's 2018 HP laptop in Thornlie. Before the upgrade, it took 3 minutes and 15 seconds just to load the Windows desktop. After installing a 1TB SSD, it booted in 11 seconds.

What you will notice immediately:

  • 1
    Lightning fast boot times: Press the power button and be at your login screen in seconds, not minutes.
  • 2
    Instant app launches: Programs like Word, Excel, Chrome, and Photoshop open almost the moment you click them.
  • 3
    Seamless updates: Windows updates install significantly faster and do not hijack your computer for hours.
  • 4
    Silent operation: Because there are no spinning disks, your laptop or desktop will run much quieter.

What happens to all my files and programs

This is the most common concern I hear. The short answer: You do not lose anything.

When you get an SSD upgrade, we perform a process called cloning. We make an exact 1 to 1 digital copy of your old hard drive and transfer it to the new SSD. When you turn your computer back on, your desktop background, your passwords, your photos, and all your software will be exactly where you left them, they will just load a lot faster.

Is my PC too old for an SSD

Probably not. Almost every laptop and desktop manufactured in the last 12 years uses a standard SATA connection, which is fully compatible with modern 2.5 inch SSDs. If your PC is running Windows 10 or 11 but feels unbearably slow, it is a prime candidate for an upgrade.

Important Note: If your laptop is heavily physically damaged, or if the processor (CPU) was extremely low end even 10 years ago (like an Intel Celeron or Atom), an SSD will help, but it might be more cost effective to replace the machine. I always offer honest advice on whether an upgrade is actually worth your money.

Is it better to just buy a new computer

A brand new laptop of decent quality will cost you anywhere from $800 to $1,500. By comparison, an SSD upgrade (including the drive itself and the labour to clone your data) generally costs a fraction of that price and yields similar performance for everyday tasks like web browsing, emails, and office work.

If your computer has a good screen, a comfortable keyboard, and is not falling apart at the hinges, an SSD upgrade is the smartest, most cost effective way to extend its lifespan by another 3 to 5 years.
