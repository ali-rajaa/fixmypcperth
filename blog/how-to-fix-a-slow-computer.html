How to Fix a Slow Computer: 10 Instant Speed Boosts

Ali Raja · FixMyPC Perth · 21 May 2026 · 8 min read · 📍 Canning Vale, Perth

Is your computer taking forever to boot? Are apps freezing while you're trying to work? You aren't alone. In Canning Vale and across Perth, slow computer performance is the #1 reason people call me for help. Windows 10 and 11 are powerful, but they accumulate clutter quickly.

Before you get frustrated and buy a new laptop at JB Hi-Fi, try these 10 troubleshooting steps to reclaim your speed instantly. These are the same professional-grade checks I perform at my workshop.

1. Audit Your Startup Programs

Many apps like Spotify, Steam, or Zoom force themselves to start the moment you turn on your PC. This eats up your RAM before you even open a browser.

The Fix: Press Ctrl + Shift + Esc to open Task Manager, click the "Startup" tab, and disable anything you don't need immediately.

2. Switch to "Best Performance" Power Mode

By default, laptops are set to "Balanced" mode to save battery, which actively throttles your CPU speed. If you're plugged into power, this is unnecessary.

Go to Settings > System > Power & Battery and change the Power Mode to "Best Performance." You'll notice windows and menus feel snappier immediately.

Pro Tip: If your fans are screaming while you do this, your PC might be overheating. In the Perth summer, dust buildup can cause "thermal throttling" where your PC slows down to stay cool.

3. Clean Up Temporary Files

Windows stores "cache" files that are supposed to help, but when they grow to 20GB+, they do the opposite. Press Win + R, type %temp%, and delete everything in that folder. Don't worry—Windows won't let you delete anything actually in use.

4. The HDD vs. SSD Factor

If your PC is more than a few years old and still uses a mechanical Hard Drive (HDD), no software fix will truly make it "fast." Upgrading to a Solid State Drive (SSD) is like moving from a bicycle to a Ferrari. It is the single most effective way to fix a slow computer.

5. Scan for Malware and PUPs

Potentially Unwanted Programs (PUPs) often bundle themselves with free downloads. They aren't always "viruses," but they sit in the background and track your data, hogging your internet and CPU.

6. Check Disk Health

If your PC freezes for 30 seconds and then recovers, your drive might be failing. You can check this by typing "cmd" in search, right-clicking it to run as Administrator, and typing chkdsk. If it finds errors, back up your data immediately.

7. Pause Windows Update Loops

Sometimes Windows gets stuck trying to install an update in the background. If you see a red icon in your taskbar or "Update failed" in settings, your PC is wasting 20% of its power trying to fix that loop.

8. Adjust Visual Effects

Windows has a lot of fancy animations and transparency effects. On older hardware, these cause lag. Search for "Adjust the appearance and performance of Windows" and select "Adjust for best performance."

9. Re-Apply Thermal Paste

If your PC is 3+ years old, the factory thermal paste on the processor has likely dried out. This leads to overheating, which forces the PC to slow down to prevent damage. Fresh paste is a cheap way to restore factory speeds.

10. Fresh Windows Reinstall

When all else fails, a "Clean Install" of Windows 11 is the nuclear option. It wipes away every piece of junk, every registry error, and every bit of malware. It makes the software side of your PC brand new.

Warning: Always ensure you have a full backup of your photos and documents before attempting a fresh install or any major disk repairs.
