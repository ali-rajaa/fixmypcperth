How to Fix a Slow Computer: 10 Instant Speed Boosts for 2026

📅 May 21, 2026 ⏱️ 8 min read 📍 Perth, WA

We’ve all been there. You sit down to start work in your Perth home office, you press the power button, and... you wait. And wait. A slow computer isn't just annoying; it’s a massive drain on your productivity. Whether you’re running Windows 10 or Windows 11, performance degradation is inevitable over time as software gets heavier and system clutter builds up.

Before you consider spending $1,500+ on a new machine at JB Hi-Fi, try these professional-grade troubleshooting steps. These are the exact same things I check first when a client drops off a PC at my Canning Vale workshop.

1. Audit Your Startup Impact

When you boot your PC, dozens of invisible programs are fighting for your RAM. Apps like Microsoft Teams, Spotify, and Adobe Updater are notorious for this. The Fix: Right-click your Taskbar > Task Manager > Startup Apps. Look at the "Startup Impact" column. Disable anything marked "High" that you don't use every single day.

2. Deep Clean Your "Temporary" Junk

Windows stores "cache" files that are supposed to make things faster, but when they grow to 20GB or 30GB, they do the opposite. The Pro Tip: Press Win + R, type %temp%, and hit enter. Select everything and delete it. Don't worry—Windows won't let you delete anything actually in use.

3. Switch Your Power Plan to "Best Performance"

By default, laptops and many desktops are set to "Balanced" mode to save power. This actively throttles your CPU speed. The Fix: Go to Settings > System > Power & Battery > Power Mode. Change this to "Best Performance." You'll notice windows and menus snappier immediately.

4. The HDD vs. SSD "Wall"

I cannot stress this enough: If your Windows 10/11 is running on a mechanical spinning hard drive (HDD), no software trick in the world will make it fast. Upgrading to a Solid State Drive (SSD) is like moving from a bicycle to a Ferrari. I perform SSD clones weekly for Perth customers—it's the best $80–$150 you can spend on your tech.

5. Check for "Ghost" Chrome Extensions

If your PC is fast but browsing the web is slow, your extensions are the culprit. Ad-blockers, VPN toggles, and "honey" coupons eat up CPU cycles. Disable every extension you haven't used in the last month.

6. Reapply Thermal Paste (The "Perth Summer" Fix)

In WA, our heat is brutal. If your PC fans are screaming, your CPU is likely "Thermal Throttling"—meaning it slows itself down so it doesn't melt. If your PC is 2+ years old, the factory thermal paste has likely dried up. Fresh high-quality paste can drop temps by 15°C and restore lost speed.

7. Pause Windows Update Loops

Often, a "Slow PC" is actually just Windows trying (and failing) to install an update in the background. Go to Windows Update settings. If you see a red "Retry" button, your PC is wasting 30% of its power trying to fix that loop. Clear the update cache to reset it.

8. Scan for "PUPs" (Potentially Unwanted Programs)

These aren't full viruses, but they are "parasite" apps that come bundled with free software. They track your data and slow your boot times. Using a tool like Malwarebytes (or booking a professional scan) is essential.
