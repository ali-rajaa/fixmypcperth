How Long Does a Laptop Screen Replacement Take?

For most common laptop brands and models, a screen replacement is completed the same day. We keep panels in stock for many popular models from Dell, HP, Lenovo, Acer, and ASUS. For less common models or specialist displays — high-resolution IPS panels, touch screens, or older models — we may need to order the part, which typically adds one to three business days.

The physical replacement process itself takes between 30 minutes and 90 minutes depending on the model. Laptops vary significantly in how accessible the screen assembly is. Some are straightforward. Others involve removing the entire lid housing and carefully routing new cables. Either way, the job is done properly the first time, and you collect a fully tested, working laptop.

Before you drop off: Send us a photo of the damage via WhatsApp and your laptop model (found on a sticker on the base). We can usually confirm parts availability and give you a fixed price quote before you make the trip to Canning Vale.

Signs Your Laptop Screen Needs Replacing

Not every screen problem looks the same. Here are the most common symptoms that indicate a screen replacement is needed, as opposed to a software or graphics driver issue which would not be fixed by a hardware repair.

  • ✓ Visible cracks, fractures, or shattered glass on the screen surface
  • ✓ Black or dark spots spreading from an impact point
  • ✓ Vertical or horizontal lines running across the display
  • ✓ Flickering or intermittent display, especially when the lid is moved
  • ✓ Half the screen is dark or showing distorted colours
  • ✓ The backlight works but the screen shows no image
  • ✓ Bright white or yellow patches that were not there before

If your laptop displays correctly when connected to an external monitor via HDMI but looks wrong on its own screen, that confirms the panel itself is faulty rather than the graphics card or Windows. This is one of the quickest diagnostic checks we run before quoting a screen replacement.

Other Common Physical Laptop Repairs in Perth

A cracked screen gets the most attention, but it is far from the only physical repair worth doing on a laptop. Here are the other jobs we handle regularly, along with what to look for and what to expect from each repair.

Broken or Stiff Laptop Hinges

Hinges take a lot of stress over a laptop's life. Every time you open and close the lid, the hinge mechanism flexes. On cheaper laptops especially, the plastic housing around the hinge cracks over time, and the hinge itself can loosen, stiffen, or break completely. A broken hinge is not just cosmetic. It puts tension on the display cable that runs through the lid, which can cause intermittent flickering or a dead screen that is often mistaken for a panel failure.

A proper hinge repair involves disassembling the lid, replacing the hinge assembly, and inspecting the cable for wear. A bodged fix with tape or glue will last weeks before failing again. A proper repair lasts the life of the machine.

Keyboard and Touchpad Replacement

Laptop keyboards take more physical abuse than almost any other component. Keys wear out, stop registering, or snap off. Spilled liquid causes individual keys to stick or fail. In some models, a single failed key can be replaced, but for most modern slim laptops the entire keyboard unit needs to be swapped as one piece. Labour starts from $100, and most keyboard replacements are completed the same day. If you are a touch typist who relies on a responsive keyboard for work, a worn-out keyboard is genuinely worth fixing rather than tolerating.

Liquid Spill and Water Damage

This is the repair where time matters most. If you have just spilled liquid on your laptop, turn it off immediately — do not let it finish booting, do not try to save your work. Hold the power button until it shuts off, flip it upside down to drain what you can, and bring it to us as soon as possible. The difference between bringing it in within an hour and leaving it overnight can be the difference between a full recovery and a permanently damaged motherboard.

When a laptop comes in after a spill, we disassemble it fully, clean the board with the appropriate solution, and let it dry properly before reassembling and testing. This is a very different process from simply letting it air dry on a bench, which leaves residue on the contacts that causes corrosion and failures weeks later.

Important: Do not put your laptop in rice. Rice does not absorb liquid from circuit boards and wastes valuable time. Get it to a professional as quickly as possible. The faster the board is cleaned, the better the chance of saving it — and your data along with it.

Charging Port Replacement

A loose or intermittent charging port is one of the most common issues on laptops that are two or more years old. You find yourself wiggling the cable at a specific angle to get it to charge, or the laptop only charges if it is sitting perfectly flat. This is a solvable problem. Charging port replacement typically takes a few hours and restores reliable charging completely. Left unfixed, a loose port will eventually stop making contact entirely and leave you with a laptop that cannot charge at all.

Is It Worth Repairing a Cracked Laptop Screen or Buying New?

This is the question worth answering honestly rather than just pitching a repair. The answer depends on three things: the age of the laptop, the cost of the screen repair relative to a replacement machine, and the overall condition of the rest of the laptop.

As a general rule, if the laptop is less than five years old, the processor and RAM are adequate for your needs, and the repair cost is less than 40 percent of a comparable replacement machine, the repair is almost always the better financial decision. You also keep your existing data, your installed software, and your familiar setup — none of which come with a new laptop out of the box.

Where we would advise against repair is when the laptop is six or more years old, already running slowly due to outdated hardware, and the repair cost combined with the cost of other likely upcoming repairs approaches the cost of a new machine. In those cases, we will tell you honestly. We are not here to sell repairs that do not make sense for your situation.
