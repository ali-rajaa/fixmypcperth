Why IT Failures Hit Trades Businesses Hard

A retail store can serve customers while a computer is being repaired. A trades or construction business cannot. When your estimating software is down, you cannot quote. When your job management platform is inaccessible, your team does not know where they are supposed to be or what they are supposed to do. When the accounting software loses its connection to the bank feed, invoicing stalls and cash flow suffers.

Trades businesses also operate across multiple locations simultaneously. Your office admin might be managing schedules on one device while your site supervisor needs to upload photos and sign off on a variation from a tablet on the other side of Perth. Both need reliable connectivity, access to the same cloud files and software that simply works. Managing that complexity without a proper IT support arrangement in place is harder than it needs to be.

The other reality is that cyber threats are no longer a concern reserved for large corporations. Small construction businesses hold sensitive information including client contact details, financial records, subcontractor agreements and compliance documentation. A ransomware attack on a trades business is not a theoretical risk. It happens, and the recovery cost without a proper backup in place is devastating.

The Most Common IT Problems We See in Perth Trades Businesses

After working with trades and construction businesses across Perth and the southern suburbs, the same issues come up repeatedly. Understanding them is the first step to making sure they stop happening to your business.

  • !Job management software going down on peak days, usually because of a Windows update conflict or a network misconfiguration that was never properly set up in the first place
  • !EFTPOS and payment terminal connectivity issues caused by router firmware that has not been updated or wifi networks that were set up years ago and never properly secured
  • !Cloud file access problems where staff in the field cannot open drawings, photos or documents because the sync settings were never configured correctly across devices
  • !Slow or unreliable computers in the office that slow down quoting, scheduling and invoicing and cost hours of productivity every single week
  • !No real backup system meaning that if a device is lost, stolen or damaged on site, the data on it is gone permanently
  • !New device setup taking too long when a new apprentice or office staff member starts, because nobody has documented what software needs to be installed or how accounts should be configured

Every one of these problems is preventable with the right IT support in place. None of them should be absorbing your time or your team's time to resolve.
