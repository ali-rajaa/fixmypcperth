Why This Matters

A Backup Is Not Something You Set Up After Disaster Strikes

Most people only think seriously about backup the moment it is already too late. A hard drive fails, a laptop is stolen from a car, a phone is dropped in the pool, or a ransomware infection locks every file on the machine. By that point, the only thing left to do is hope a backup already exists somewhere. For far too many Perth households and small businesses, it does not.

What makes this particularly frustrating is how avoidable it is. A proper backup, set up correctly once, runs quietly in the background from that point forward without you needing to think about it again. The people who lose everything are almost never the ones who set up a backup and decided it was not worth maintaining. They are the ones who always meant to get around to it.

Hard Drives Fail Without Warning
Mechanical and even solid state drives have a limited lifespan. Most give little to no warning before they stop working completely.
Laptops Get Lost or Stolen
A stolen laptop is a frustrating financial loss. A stolen laptop with the only copy of your files is a far bigger problem entirely.
Ransomware Targets Your Files Directly
Modern ransomware specifically seeks out and encrypts documents and photos. A separate backup is often the only real protection against it.
Accidental Deletion Happens to Everyone
A wrong click, an accidental format, or a careless drag and drop has wiped out years of files for plenty of careful, sensible people.
Liquid Damage and Physical Accidents
Spilled drinks, drops, and water damage are some of the most common reasons Perth customers come to us needing data recovery, often unsuccessfully.
You Cannot Recreate Memories
Lost documents can sometimes be rewritten. Lost family photos and videos from years gone by simply cannot be replaced at any price.

How It Works

Cloud Backup and Local Backup, Explained Simply

There are two main types of backup, and the strongest protection comes from using both together. Here is the difference in plain language, without the technical jargon that usually makes this topic more confusing than it needs to be.

Cloud Backup Your files are copied securely over the internet to a remote storage service. Protects you even if your computer is stolen, damaged, or destroyed entirely, since the copy lives somewhere else.
Off Site
Local Backup Your files are copied to a separate physical device, typically an external hard drive. Gives you a fast offline copy that does not depend on an internet connection to restore.
Physical Copy
Automatic Scheduling Once configured, backup runs in the background on a regular schedule without you needing to remember to do anything manually.
Hands Off
Using Both Together Cloud backup protects against theft, fire, and physical loss. Local backup protects against a slow internet connection and gives you a fast local restore. Together, they cover almost every realistic scenario.
