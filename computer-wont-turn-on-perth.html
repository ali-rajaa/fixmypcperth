Why This Matters

A Computer That Wont Turn On Is Not Something to Sit On

When a computer or laptop suddenly stops turning on, it is easy to assume the worst, or to assume nothing at all and just keep trying the power button hoping it sorts itself out. Both reactions can cost you. A machine that will not start can be something minor that is quick and cheap to put right, or it can be a sign of a more serious fault that gets worse the longer it is left unchecked.

The honest truth is that without opening the machine up and properly testing it, nobody can tell you over the phone or from a photo whether your computer has a simple fix or a serious one. What we can tell you is that getting it looked at properly, and quickly, gives you the best chance of a fast and affordable outcome. Many Perth customers wait days or weeks before bringing in a machine that wont turn on, hoping it will resolve itself. It almost never does, and by the time it arrives with us the underlying issue has often had longer to cause additional damage.

No Lights, No Sound, Nothing at All
A completely dead machine with zero response when you press power needs an electrical and component level check to identify exactly where the fault sits.
Stuck on the Manufacturer Logo
Power is reaching the machine but it cannot progress past the startup screen. This points to a software or boot related issue that needs a proper look.
Stuck in a Restart Loop
The machine powers on, then shuts down or restarts itself repeatedly without ever fully starting. This pattern can have several different underlying causes.
Fans Spin But Screen Stays Black
Hearing the fans run while the screen shows nothing usually points toward a display, graphics or connection fault rather than a complete power failure.
Worked Fine, Then Suddenly Did Not
A sudden failure with no warning, especially after a storm, a power outage or being moved, is a strong reason to get it checked sooner rather than later.
Intermittent Power, On and Off
A machine that sometimes turns on and sometimes does not is often more concerning than one that has failed completely, as it points to an unstable fault.

  • Your data is almost always still intact and recoverable, even when the computer itself will not start

Possible Causes

What Can Cause a Computer to Stop Turning On

There is no single reason a computer or laptop stops turning on, which is exactly why guessing at home rarely solves anything and can occasionally make things worse. Below are the categories of fault we most commonly diagnose in Perth. We are not covering how to test or fix any of these yourself. The point is simply to show why a proper diagnostic, rather than a guess, is the only reliable way forward.

Power Supply Failure The component responsible for delivering power to the rest of the machine has failed or is delivering unstable power.
Diagnosed
Motherboard Fault Damage or failure on the motherboard itself, sometimes caused by a power surge, age, or a failed component elsewhere.
Diagnosed
RAM or Memory Issues Failing or dislodged memory modules can prevent a system from completing its startup sequence entirely.
Diagnosed
Battery or Charging Faults On laptops, a failed battery or damaged charging port and circuit is one of the most common causes of no power symptoms.
Diagnosed
Corrupted Boot Files Software level damage to the files Windows needs to start can cause a machine to get stuck on the logo or loop endlessly.
Diagnosed
Failing Storage Drive A drive that is dying can prevent Windows from loading even though the rest of the machine is otherwise healthy.
Diagnosed
Display or Graphics Fault Sometimes the machine is actually starting correctly but a display or graphics issue makes it appear completely dead.
Diagnosed

What Happens If a Replacement Part Is Needed

Plenty of no power and no boot issues are resolved through the diagnostic and repair process alone, without any new parts. But some genuinely do need a replacement component, whether that is a power supply, motherboard, battery, charging port or another internal part. We would rather tell you exactly what that means up front than have you assume your machine will be ready the next day and then be disappointed.

Once a diagnostic identifies that a part is required, we confirm the exact part needed and quote you the cost separately from the $120 diagnostic and labour fee. We then move to sourcing it. Common parts for popular brands are often available within a few business days. Less common parts, older models, or components for less widely available machines can take up to ten business days to arrive, depending on supplier stock. We will give you our best estimate as soon as we know what is needed, and we will keep you updated if anything changes along the way.

Your machine stays safely with us while we wait on the part, and we do not begin sourcing anything until you have approved the cost. There is never a surprise bill waiting for you at collection.
