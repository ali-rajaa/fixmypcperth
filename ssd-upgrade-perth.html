Is This Your PC?

Signs Your Computer Needs an SSD Upgrade

Old mechanical hard drives slow down significantly over time and become the single biggest bottleneck in an otherwise capable PC. If your computer is more than four or five years old and still has its original hard drive, the drive is almost certainly the reason it feels slow. Here are the signs that an SSD upgrade is exactly what your machine needs.

Boot Time Over Two Minutes
A healthy mechanical drive boots Windows in 60 to 90 seconds. If you are waiting two, three or four minutes from pressing the power button to a usable desktop, the drive is the problem.
Programs Hang When Opening
Chrome, Word, or your email client taking 15 to 30 seconds to open is a sign the drive cannot keep up with read requests. On an SSD, the same programs open in one to three seconds.
Windows Updates Take Hours
Updates that take half a day to install are usually bottlenecked by a slow drive writing update files. The same updates on an SSD complete in a fraction of the time.
Clicking or Grinding Sounds
Any clicking, grinding or repetitive ticking from inside the computer is a mechanical hard drive under stress. This is a warning sign of imminent failure and should be addressed immediately before data is lost.
PC Struggles With Basic Tasks
If your PC feels sluggish just browsing the web or writing documents, and you have ruled out virus infection and too many startup programs, the hard drive is almost certainly the cause.
Drive Errors in Windows
Error messages about drive health, requests to run CHKDSK, or frequent freezes while saving files all indicate drive degradation. Upgrading before it fails completely protects your data.

Our Process

How We Do the SSD Upgrade

We clone your existing drive to the new SSD rather than doing a fresh Windows install. This means everything on your current computer, including Windows, all your programs, all your files, your browser bookmarks, your desktop layout and your settings, comes across exactly as it was. You collect a PC that feels like a new machine but looks exactly how you left it.

1
Drive Health Check Before We Start
Before cloning anything we run a health check on your existing hard drive using CrystalDiskInfo. This tells us whether the drive is healthy enough for a clean clone or whether it has errors that need addressing first. If the drive is failing we tell you before proceeding and discuss your options, including data recovery if necessary.
2
Confirm the Right SSD for Your Machine
We verify the correct SSD type for your specific laptop or desktop model. Different machines use different interfaces including 2.5 inch SATA, M.2 SATA and M.2 NVMe. Getting this wrong means the drive will not fit or will not be detected. We confirm this before sourcing or fitting anything.
3
Full Data Clone to the New SSD
We use professional cloning software to copy your entire existing drive to the new SSD sector by sector. Windows, programs, files, settings and all. The clone is verified after completion to confirm it is bootable and complete. This typically takes one to three hours depending on how much data is on the current drive.
4
SSD Installed, Old Drive Kept or Removed
The new SSD is fitted into the machine. If your laptop or desktop has a spare drive bay, we can install the old hard drive as secondary storage so you keep all that extra space. If there is no spare bay, we return the old drive to you separately so you still have it as a backup. We let you decide what you want done with the old drive.
5
Speed Test and Handover
We boot Windows from the new SSD, time the boot, confirm everything opens correctly and run a quick benchmark to verify the SSD is performing at full speed. You collect a machine that has been tested and confirmed working. We show you the before and after boot times so you can see the improvement for yourself.
