Tech explained simply

Why is my computer so slow?
A practical guide.

Ageing Hard Drive

Old mechanical drives degrade over time, causing boot times to hit minutes. Upgrading to an SSD fundamentally changes how your machine feels—making it faster than the day you bought it.

Background Malware

Not all malware is loud. Many infections run silently, consuming your CPU and RAM for crypto-mining or data harvesting. If your PC is slow and hot for no reason, this is a likely culprit.

Windows Corruption

Years of software updates and registry changes leave "bloat" that slows down even fast hardware. A clean Windows reinstall resets the environment, giving you a fresh start.

Insufficient RAM

Modern browsers are RAM-hungry. If you have 4GB or 8GB of RAM, your PC is likely struggling to keep up with today's apps. Upgrading your memory allows for smooth multitasking.

Virus and malware

How to tell if your computer
has a virus.

Hijacked Browser

Homepages changing, random search redirects, and new toolbars appearing are classic signs of browser malware. These threats often resist standard settings and require professional removal.

High Idle Usage

If your Task Manager shows high CPU or RAM usage when you aren't doing anything, malware could be running silently in the background, mining crypto or harvesting data.

Disabled Security

Sophisticated infections often disable Windows Defender or your antivirus first. If your security software won't open or stays turned off, your system is likely compromised.

Ghost Messages

If friends report receiving strange emails or messages from you that you didn't send, your credentials have likely been harvested and are being used by third parties.
