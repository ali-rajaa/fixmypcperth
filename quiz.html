Why is my computer so slow? A Perth tech's honest guide

This is the number one question we get from customers across Perth, and the answer is almost always one of four things. Understanding which one applies to your situation can save you a lot of time and money.

1. Your hard drive is old and needs replacing with an SSD

If your PC is more than four years old and uses a traditional spinning hard drive (HDD), this is almost certainly why it's slow. Old hard drives are 5–10 times slower than modern solid state drives (SSDs), and as they age, they slow down further. Boot times of 5 minutes or more, programs taking forever to open, and general sluggishness during everyday tasks are all classic signs.

An SSD upgrade is the single most cost-effective repair we perform. After the upgrade, most customers report their computers feeling like brand new machines — boot times typically drop from several minutes to under 15 seconds, and programs open almost instantly. All your files, programs, and settings are transferred over during the process, so you don't lose anything.

2. Malware or a virus is running in the background

Malware doesn't always announce itself with pop-ups. Many modern infections run silently in the background, consuming CPU, memory and internet bandwidth while you're trying to work. If your PC has slowed down noticeably, has a fan that runs constantly, or uses a lot of CPU when nothing seems to be open, malware is a real possibility.

Consumer antivirus tools like Windows Defender, Malwarebytes and Norton catch a lot — but not everything. Deeply embedded rootkits, spyware and adware often survive a standard scan. A professional removal service uses multiple tools, boot-time scanning, and manual inspection to make sure the system is clean.

3. Windows has become corrupted or bloated over time

Every time you install and uninstall software, Windows leaves behind traces — broken registry entries, orphaned files, conflicting drivers, and startup programs that accumulate over years. Eventually, this builds up to the point where Windows itself is the bottleneck. A clean Windows reinstall (with your data backed up first) is often the fastest fix for a PC that's been in use for several years without ever being wiped.

4. You don't have enough RAM for what you're doing

Modern browsers like Chrome can use gigabytes of RAM on their own. If your PC only has 4GB of RAM — common in computers sold between 2012 and 2018 — you may simply be running out of memory when multiple tabs or programs are open. Upgrading from 4GB to 8GB or 16GB of RAM is a straightforward, affordable fix that makes multitasking dramatically faster.

Signs your computer might have a virus — and what to do

Malware has evolved significantly. The pop-up-heavy, obvious infections of the early 2000s have been replaced by sophisticated software that hides itself effectively. Here are the signs Perth residents most commonly report when they have an infection:

  • Your browser homepage has changed to something you didn't set
  • You're being redirected to unfamiliar websites when clicking search results
  • Random pop-up ads appear even when your browser is closed
  • Your PC is noticeably slower than it was a few weeks or months ago
  • Task Manager shows high CPU or memory usage but you have nothing open
  • New browser toolbars or extensions have appeared without you installing them
  • Your antivirus has been disabled or you can't open it
  • Friends are receiving strange emails or messages from your accounts

If you recognise two or more of these, the quiz above will help pinpoint the likely culprit. Don't ignore a potential malware infection — many modern infections are designed to steal saved passwords, banking credentials, and personal information. The sooner it's removed, the less exposure you have.
