Do You Recognise This?

Signs You May Have Lost Important Data

Data loss happens without warning. A single click, a power cut, a dropped laptop, or a failing drive can put years of photos, documents, and business files out of reach. These are the situations Perth customers bring to us most often. If any of these sound familiar, do not write off your data before speaking to us.

Files or Folders Accidentally Deleted
Emptied the recycle bin, deleted the wrong folder, or lost files after a Windows reinstall. Deleted data is often fully recoverable if you act quickly.
Hard Drive Not Detected or Not Spinning
Your computer no longer recognises the drive, or you can hear clicking, grinding, or no sound at all. Stop using it immediately and bring it in for assessment.
Drive Shows as Unformatted or Corrupted
Windows is asking you to format the drive before you can use it, or files appear with no names and garbled characters. Do not format. Your data is still there.
Laptop or PC Crashed and Will Not Boot
System failure after a crash, failed Windows update, or power cut. The operating system is gone but the underlying data on your drive is usually fully intact.
SSD or USB Drive Stopped Working
A USB flash drive, external SSD, or internal SSD is not being recognised or shows as empty. Flash storage failure requires specialist tools to recover successfully.
SD Card Not Readable or Shows Wrong Size
Camera or phone SD card that is not detected, showing incorrect storage size, or appearing empty after a card error. Photos and videos on SD cards are frequently recoverable.
Files Lost After a Virus or Ransomware Attack
Ransomware has encrypted your files or a virus has deleted them. Depending on the infection type, partial or full recovery may still be possible alongside virus removal.
Drive Making Unusual Noises
Clicking, beeping, or scraping sounds from a hard drive indicate mechanical failure. Turn the machine off immediately. Continuing to run a failing drive reduces the chance of successful recovery.
Act fast to protect your data
The single most important thing you can do after data loss is stop using the affected device immediately. Every read and write operation to the drive risks overwriting the files you are trying to recover. Do not attempt to copy files, run a scan, or reinstall Windows on the affected drive before speaking to us.

Prevention

How to Protect Your Data and Avoid Losing It Again

The best data recovery job is the one you never need. After every recovery job we take the time to help you set up a proper backup strategy so this situation does not happen again. Here is what we recommend to every Perth customer.

Use the 3-2-1 backup rule. Keep three copies of your important data, on two different types of storage, with one copy kept offsite or in the cloud. For most home users this means your computer, an external hard drive, and a cloud backup service such as Google Drive, OneDrive, or Backblaze. This covers you against drive failure, theft, fire, and accidental deletion simultaneously.

Enable automatic backups. Manual backups are rarely done consistently. Windows Backup, File History, and third-party tools like Macrium Reflect can back up your files automatically on a schedule without you needing to remember. We set this up for customers after every recovery job at no extra charge.

Do not ignore a drive that is behaving strangely. Clicking sounds, slow performance, frequent errors, or files disappearing are all early warning signs of drive failure. A drive that is failing but still functional is the best possible time to recover your data. Do not wait until it stops working entirely.

Invest in an SSD if you are still running an old hard drive. Mechanical hard drives have moving parts that wear out over time. An SSD upgrade eliminates most mechanical failure risk, makes your computer significantly faster, and provides much more reliable long-term storage. We combine data recovery with SSD upgrades regularly at a combined rate.

Keep your operating system and antivirus up to date. Viruses and malware remain one of the leading causes of data loss in Perth homes and businesses. Combined with regular backups, keeping your machine protected with proper antivirus software removes the most common preventable data loss risk. Read more about virus and malware removal if you suspect an active infection.
