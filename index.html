If your PC is slow and unresponsive, first try restarting and checking Task Manager (Ctrl+Shift+Esc) for anything using 100% CPU or disk. If that doesn't help, run a free Malwarebytes scan to rule out malware. If it's still slow, the most likely cause is an old spinning hard drive — an SSD upgrade at FixMyPC Perth starts from $65 and most customers say it makes the computer feel brand new.
To fix a slow computer yourself: open Task Manager and disable startup programs you don't need, free up storage space (aim for at least 15% free on your C: drive), run a Malwarebytes scan for malware, and check your CPU temperature isn't throttling due to dust. If none of that works, the drive itself is likely failing or too slow — bring it to us in Canning Vale for a free diagnostic.
