Why Is My Computer So Slow? The Real Causes

"Why is my computer so slow" and "why is my PC so slow" are two of the most common things people search before they ever think about a repair shop — and honestly, in a lot of cases the fix doesn't need a technician at all. Here are the seven causes we see most often when diagnosing a slow PC or laptop in Perth:

  • Too many startup programs. Software you installed months ago is often still loading every time you turn the computer on, quietly eating RAM and CPU before you've opened a single window.
  • A nearly full hard drive. Once a drive — especially an older spinning hard disk — gets above about 85% full, Windows slows down significantly because it has nowhere to write temporary files.
  • Malware or adware running in the background. Infections don't always show obvious symptoms like pop-ups; sometimes the only sign is a computer that's mysteriously sluggish, especially when browsing.
  • Not enough RAM for what you're doing. If you've got a browser open with 30 tabs, Office, and a video call all running on 8GB of RAM, the computer is constantly swapping memory to disk — and that's painfully slow.
  • An old spinning hard drive instead of an SSD. This is the single biggest factor we see. A computer with a traditional hard drive can take two to three minutes to boot; the same computer with an SSD often boots in under fifteen seconds.
  • Overheating. Dust build-up inside laptops and desktops causes the CPU to throttle its own speed to avoid overheating — which feels exactly like a slow computer, because it is one (deliberately, by design).
  • A Windows installation that's accumulated years of junk. Registry bloat, leftover driver versions, failed updates and corrupted system files all add up. Sometimes the cleanest fix really is a fresh Windows install with your files backed up first.

If you want to dig into this in more detail, our full guide on why your PC is slow walks through how to check each of these yourself using Task Manager and free tools, before you spend a cent on a repair.

How to Fix a Slow Computer Yourself (Before You Book a Repair)

If your computer is slow and unresponsive, here's the order we'd check things in, starting with the free options:

  • Open Task Manager (Ctrl + Shift + Esc) and sort by CPU and Memory to see what's actually using your resources right now.
  • Go into Startup Apps and disable anything you don't recognise or don't need running constantly.
  • Check your free disk space — aim to keep at least 15-20% of your main drive free.
  • Run a full scan with a reputable free tool like Malwarebytes to rule out malware.
  • Check Windows Update is fully up to date — sometimes a stuck update is silently consuming resources.

If you've done all of that and the computer is still slow, the hardware itself is usually the limiting factor — most commonly an old hard drive or insufficient RAM. That's where an SSD upgrade (from $65 labour, you can supply the drive or we can) makes the biggest difference of almost any single repair we do. Data Recovery Perth: What to Do If Your Drive Has Failed

If you're searching data recovery Perth or "signs your hard drive is failing," the most important thing is to stop using the drive immediately. Continuing to use a failing hard drive — especially one making clicking, grinding or beeping noises — can turn a recoverable situation into a permanent one. Common warning signs of a failing hard drive include unusual noises, files that suddenly become corrupted or won't open, the computer freezing during file transfers, and increasingly frequent "blue screen" crashes.
