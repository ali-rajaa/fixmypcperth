Screen Damage Signs

Signs Your Laptop Screen Needs Replacing

Not all screen problems look the same. A cracked screen is obvious, but there are plenty of less obvious faults that are equally worth fixing. If you recognise any of the following, your screen or display cable likely needs attention and we can diagnose the exact cause before quoting you anything.

Cracked or Shattered Screen
Physical damage to the LCD panel from drops, pressure or impacts. Even if the laptop still partly works, a cracked screen will worsen over time and can damage the display cable beneath.
Completely Black Screen
The laptop powers on, you can hear it running, but nothing appears on screen. This is often a failed backlight, a dead LCD panel, or a loose display cable that needs reseating or replacement.
Vertical or Horizontal Lines
Coloured or white lines running across the screen indicate LCD panel damage or a failing display connection. These lines typically spread over time if left unrepaired.
Screen Flickering
Intermittent flickering when the lid is moved to certain angles usually points to a damaged display cable. Constant flickering regardless of position often indicates a faulty panel or inverter.
Dark or Dim Display
If the image is visible but very faint even at full brightness, the backlight has likely failed. You may be able to see the image faintly by shining a torch at the screen, which confirms a backlight fault.
Colour Distortion or Dead Pixels
Large patches of wrong colour, permanently white or black pixels, or blotchy discolouration all indicate LCD panel failure. These faults do not resolve on their own and require a screen replacement.

Our Process

What Happens When You Drop Off Your Laptop

Every screen replacement at FixMyPC Perth follows the same careful process. We do not just swap the panel and hand it back. We make sure the replacement is correct, fitted properly, and working perfectly before you leave.

1
Diagnosis. Cable or Screen?
Before ordering anything we confirm the exact fault. Some symptoms that look like a dead screen are actually caused by a loose or damaged display cable, which is cheaper and faster to fix. We connect an external monitor to the laptop to verify whether the fault is the panel, the cable, or the graphics output. You only pay for what is actually needed.
2
Confirm Model and Source the Screen
We identify the exact screen specification your laptop needs. This includes size, resolution, panel type, connector type and brightness rating. We then source the correct replacement through our trade supplier. Most screens for common Dell, HP, Lenovo, ASUS and Acer models are available same day or next day through our supplier network.
3
Full Price Confirmed Before We Start
Once we know the exact screen cost, we confirm the complete all in price with you. Labour is $120. Screen cost varies by model and resolution. We never order parts or start work without your explicit approval of the full price. No surprises at collection.
4
Screen Replaced. Bezel and Hinges Checked.
We disassemble the display assembly carefully, remove the damaged panel, and fit the replacement. We check the hinges and bezel for any additional damage at the same time. The screen is secured properly, all cables are seated correctly, and the bezel is refitted without cracking it. Sloppy disassembly that leaves cracked bezels or damaged hinges is something we take care to avoid.
5
Full Display Test Before Handover
We run the laptop through a full display test. This includes checking brightness at all levels, colour accuracy, dead pixels, backlight uniformity, and testing the screen across all lid angles to confirm there is no cable tension causing flickering. Only when the display passes every check do we confirm the job is complete and contact you for collection.
