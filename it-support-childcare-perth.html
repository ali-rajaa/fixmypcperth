Why IT Failures Are Particularly Disruptive for Childcare Centres

A childcare centre cannot pause its operations while a technical problem is resolved. Children are present, educators are occupied with supervision and care, and administrative staff are often working alone. When the childcare management system becomes unavailable, attendance records cannot be updated, CCS subsidies cannot be processed and parent communications cannot be sent through the platform. When the internet connection drops, cloud-based systems that the entire operation depends on become inaccessible simultaneously.

The regulatory dimension adds another layer of urgency that is unique to the childcare sector. The Education and Care Services National Law and the National Quality Framework impose specific requirements around documentation, incident recording and the retention of enrolment and medical information. Centres that cannot access their records during an inspection, that lose incident reports due to a system failure, or that cannot demonstrate current compliance documentation face serious consequences that go beyond the immediate operational disruption.

Child safety is also a consideration that shapes the IT requirements of a childcare centre in ways that do not apply to most other business types. Authorised collection records, custody restriction documentation, allergy and medical alerts and emergency contact information must be accessible at all times during operating hours. A system failure that makes any of that information temporarily inaccessible is not simply an inconvenience. It is a safeguarding risk.

Regulatory note: Childcare centres operating under the National Quality Framework are required to maintain accurate enrolment records, incident and injury documentation, and authorisation records for collections and medications. The Australian Privacy Act also applies to the personal and medical information centres hold about children and families. FixMyPC Perth implements encrypted backup, managed antivirus and controlled device access as standard components of every managed IT plan, directly supporting your obligations under both frameworks.

The Most Common IT Problems We See in Perth Childcare Centres

After working with childcare and early learning businesses across Perth and the southern suburbs, the same IT problems appear consistently. These are the issues that create the most disruption to daily operations and carry the highest risk if they remain unaddressed.

  • ! Childcare management software going down during operating hours
    Xplor, Kinderm8 and HiMama outages during the day are often caused by network configurations that were never properly set up, browser conflicts introduced by automatic updates, or device performance issues that have been left unresolved for months. The software gets blamed when the underlying IT environment is the actual cause.
  • ! CCMS access failures during funding periods
    The Child Care Management System requires consistent browser configurations, active digital certificates and reliable internet connectivity. When any of these fall out of alignment, CCS processing and attendance reporting stall at exactly the times they cannot afford to, causing subsidy payment delays and administrative backlogs that take hours to clear.
  • ! Shared device failures affecting multiple educators
    Tablets and shared computers used for observations, daily journals, Storypark entries and sign-in systems are often the most heavily used devices in a centre and the least regularly maintained. When a shared device fails mid-day, it removes a tool that multiple staff members depend on simultaneously.
  • ! Unreliable internet connectivity across the centre
    Centres with multiple rooms, outdoor areas and separate buildings frequently have wifi dead spots that interrupt cloud application access at inconsistent times. Routers that were installed during setup and never subsequently configured or updated are a common source of connectivity issues that appear unpredictable but are entirely preventable.
  • ! No secure backup of enrolment and compliance records
    Enrolment forms, immunisation records, medical management plans, custody documentation and incident reports stored only on a local computer or an unsynchronised folder have no protection against hardware failure or accidental deletion. Centres that lose this information face immediate compliance and legal consequences.
  • ! New staff and device setup taking too long
    Staff turnover in childcare is higher than most sectors. Each new educator who joins needs access to the management platform, observation apps, parent communication tools and shared drives configured correctly from their first day. Without a structured process, this setup falls on the centre director and consumes time that should be spent on operations and quality improvement.
